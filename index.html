Between Numbers and Narratives

Welcome.

I am a mixed-methods researcher working at the intersection of data science and economic geography. Most of my work focuses on the platformisation and datafication of the economy, and is grounded in collaborative projects with the tech industry, such as the streaming-giant Deezer. I have shared my results and recommendations in diverse avenues, from academic conferences to industry panels.

On the one hand, my quantitative analysis of user behavior data demonstrated how platforms generate a structural double movement: the increased centralization of resources alongside a simultaneous fragmentation of opportunity, thereby reconfiguring established hierarchies.

On the other hand, I conducted qualitative projects to examine a central paradox of the platformised economy: its need for state-backed legal structures to secure data value, alongside their strategic evasion of state oversight to maximize data mobility.

At its heart, my work is about translation : turning data into insight, and research into strategy for both boardrooms and lab rooms.
